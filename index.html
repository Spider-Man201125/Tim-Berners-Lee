Tim Berners-Lee is someone who invented the World Wide Web in 1989. He is the director of the World Wide Web Consortium that he founded. In 2001, he became a Fellow of the Royal SOciety and in 2004 he was knighted. In 1994, Berners founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Laboratory of Computer Science (LCS) at the Institute of Technology in Boston. In 1995, Microsoft produced the first version of Internet Explorer and this commercial browser commenced growth in websites and web users.
